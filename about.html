Who We Are

Action Pour le Développement de la Femme et de l'Enfant (APDFE) is a survivor-led, women- and child-centered humanitarian organization founded in 2019 by two human rights defenders who transformed their personal experiences of violence and loss into a powerful force for collective healing and systemic change.

We are not just an organization that works for vulnerable populations—we ARE those populations. Our staff, leadership, and community mobilizers are survivors of genocide, armed conflict, gender-based violence, displacement, and extreme poverty. This lived experience is our greatest asset and guides everything we do.

Operating in four of Central Africa's most fragile and conflict-affected countries—Central African Republic, Democratic Republic of Congo (Eastern DRC), Republic of Congo (Congo-Brazzaville), and Cameroon—we deliver trauma-informed, culturally relevant, and locally-led interventions in health, education, protection, economic empowerment, and peace-building.

Our Core Values

Survivor-Centered

We believe survivors are the most powerful agents of change. Our programs are designed BY survivors, FOR survivors.

Dignity & Respect

Every person deserves to live with dignity. We restore hope, autonomy, and self-worth to those society has marginalized.

Community-Led

Real change comes from within communities. We empower local leaders and support grassroots solutions.

Do No Harm

Safety, confidentiality, and trauma-informed care are at the heart of every intervention we design.

Our Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To empower, protect, and advocate for vulnerable women and children living in conflict and post-conflict environments across Central Africa by providing holistic, trauma-informed services in health, education, economic empowerment, child protection, environmental sustainability, and peace-building.

Our Vision

A Central Africa where every woman and child—regardless of conflict, displacement, or poverty—lives with dignity, safety, and the full realization of their rights and potential in resilient, peaceful communities.

Our Journey

Every milestone in our history represents lives transformed, communities rebuilt, and survivors who refused to let their past define their future. This is the story of how pain became purpose, and purpose became power.

2019

The Seed is Planted

A Vision Born from Survival

In a small community hall in Bangui, Central African Republic, two survivors made a promise: no woman, no child should walk the path they walked—alone, unseen, unheard. Adelithe MUGABO and Princia KORONADO officially registered APDFE with just $500, a borrowed office space, and an unshakeable belief that those who survive violence can lead the healing of others.

"We started with 3 volunteers, 1 desk, and infinite determination. By the end of 2019, we had supported 450 women and children in Bangui's most marginalized neighborhoods."
2020

Expansion Amid Crisis

Crossing Borders, Breaking Barriers

As armed violence escalated in Eastern DRC, we answered the call. Our first cross-border mission took us to North Kivu, where we opened Women's Safe Spaces in displacement camps. Despite COVID-19 lockdowns and active conflict, our team—many of them survivors themselves—delivered psychosocial support, emergency supplies, and GBV response services to 8,000+ women and children.

"COVID couldn't stop us. Conflict couldn't stop us. When others evacuated, we stayed—because survivors don't abandon survivors."
2021

From Relief to Resilience

Building Skills, Restoring Dignity

We launched our Economic Empowerment Initiative, training 1,200 women in tailoring, soap-making, and small business management. Survivors who once relied on aid started businesses, hired employees, and sent their children to school. We also trained 50 community health workers and protection officers—most of them women who had survived the very violence they now help others overcome.

"Mama Grace went from receiving emergency food aid to owning the most successful tailoring shop in her village. She now employs 6 other women. That's the power of economic dignity."
2022

A Regional Movement

Four Countries, One Mission

Our footprint expanded to Republic of Congo and Cameroon, serving refugees, IDPs, and host communities. We introduced mental health services, launched the Girls' Education Scholarship Program (supporting 800+ girls), and established mobile health clinics. By year's end, we had 32,000 direct beneficiaries and partnerships with 15 international organizations. APDFE was no longer a local NGO—it was a regional force.

"When we crossed into Cameroon, local women told us: 'We've been waiting for someone like you—someone who understands.' We didn't just bring programs—we brought sisterhood."
2023

Innovation Meets Impact

Adapting for the Future

We launched Climate-Smart Agriculture training, helping 2,500 families build resilience against drought and food insecurity. Our Peace-Building Circles brought together women from opposing ethnic groups—former enemies became allies. We introduced digital literacy programs, solar energy projects, and youth entrepreneurship hubs. Total reach: 45,000+ lives transformed across 142 communities.

"In one peace circle, two women who lost family members in the same conflict sat together, cried together, and ultimately healed together. That's when we knew peace isn't just possible—it's inevitable when women lead it."
2024

Amplifying Voices

From Survivors to Policymakers

We co-founded the Central Africa Women's Protection Network, connecting 35+ grassroots organizations across the region. APDFE staff testified before regional parliaments, advising on GBV legislation and child protection policies. Recognition poured in—but more importantly, survivors were now sitting at decision-making tables. Over 68,000 beneficiaries. 120+ staff members. Survivors leading every level of our organization.

"When survivor-leaders speak, policymakers listen differently. We're not asking for a seat at the table—we're building our own tables and inviting allies to join us."
2025

The Future is Now

Scaling What Works, Dreaming Bigger

Our goal: reach 100,000 beneficiaries by year-end through expansion of renewable energy projects, trauma healing centers, and girls' scholarships. We're launching the Survivor Leadership Academy—training the next generation of humanitarian leaders. We're piloting a Women's Cooperative Bank to provide microloans without exploitative interest rates. The future isn't something we wait for—it's something we build, one empowered woman at a time.

"We started in 2019 asking: 'Can survivors lead change?' Today, we're not asking anymore—we're proving it, every single day, in every community we serve."

Where We Work

APDFE operates in four of the world's most challenging humanitarian contexts—places where conflict, poverty, and displacement intersect to create urgent needs for women and children. Our presence in these countries is not symbolic—it's operational, deeply rooted, and community-driven. We go where others cannot or will not go.

Our Primary Hub

Central African Republic

The epicenter of our work. CAR has endured decades of instability, with ongoing armed violence affecting millions—particularly women and girls who face extreme rates of gender-based violence. We operate in Bangui and surrounding provinces, focusing on:

  • GBV response & survivor support
  • Mental health & psychosocial services
  • Child protection in conflict zones
  • Women's economic empowerment
25,000+ Reached 8 Safe Spaces Since 2019
Eastern Provinces

Democratic Republic of Congo
(Eastern DRC)

Active armed conflict in North Kivu, South Kivu, and Ituri has displaced millions and created one of the world's most severe humanitarian catastrophes. We work in the hardest-hit areas providing:

  • Emergency nutrition for malnourished children
  • Trauma healing & mental health support
  • Education for displaced children
  • Peace-building & conflict mediation
28,000+ Reached 12 Communities Since 2020
Refugee Support Hub

Republic of Congo
(Congo-Brazzaville)

Home to thousands of refugees from CAR and DRC seeking safety and a chance to rebuild. We serve both urban refugees and those in camps, offering:

  • Economic empowerment & vocational training
  • Girls' education scholarships
  • Integration & social cohesion programs
  • Legal aid & protection services
10,000+ Reached 5 Centers Since 2022
Crisis Response

Cameroon

The Anglophone crisis and Boko Haram violence in the Far North have created massive internal displacement—families torn apart, communities destroyed. We deliver critical services including:

  • Primary healthcare & mobile clinics
  • Maternal & reproductive health
  • Livelihoods & cash assistance
  • Protection for women and children
5,000+ Reached 4 Clinics Since 2022

Why Central Africa?

Central Africa hosts some of the world's longest-running humanitarian crises. These four countries share borders, populations, conflicts, and displacement patterns—creating a complex web of interconnected challenges. An integrated regional approach allows us to serve refugees, returnees, and host communities more effectively—meeting people wherever they are on their journey from survival to dignity.
